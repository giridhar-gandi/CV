Master Thesis Project (In progress)

  • Investigating hydrogen integration pathways for the sector coupled German energy system:

    I'm proud to be contributing to one of the most significant energy transitions of our time through my work on the H2CLIP project. My thesis focuses on a crucial challenge of designing optimal hydrogen supply chains between Canada and Germany to support Germany's ambitious climate goals.

    Using advanced energy system modeling, I'm analyzing how Germany can best integrate hydrogen into its existing energy infrastructure across multiple sectors - from industrial applications to transportation and heating. My research evaluates various hydrogen carriers and import options, comparing their economic viability against domestic production to create a comprehensive roadmap for Germany's hydrogen future.

    What makes this work exciting is its real-world impact. By aligning our models with actual utility and industry plans, we're developing practical solutions that could shape how nations approach their energy transitions. The findings from this research will help inform policy decisions and infrastructure investments worth billions of euros.

    This project perfectly combines my background in mechanical engineering with my passion for sustainable energy solutions, allowing me to contribute to one of the most pressing challenges of our time - the global energy transition.
